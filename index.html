About Islamic Year 2026

The Islamic Year 2026, also known as the Hijri year, is based on the lunar calendar and holds great significance for Muslims around the world. It marks the passage of time since the Prophet Muhammad's migration from Mecca to Medina in 622 CE. The year is observed with various religious and cultural practices, including fasting during Ramadan, performing Hajj pilgrimage, and celebrating Islamic holidays.

Significance of Islamic Year 2026

The Islamic Year 2026 holds immense significance for Muslims worldwide as it serves as a reminder of their faith, history, and cultural heritage. It is a time for reflection, spiritual growth, and community bonding. The year is marked by various religious observances, including fasting during Ramadan, performing Hajj pilgrimage, and celebrating Islamic holidays such as Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha.

Why Muharram is Significant

Muharram is one of the most sacred months in Islam. It marks the beginning of the Islamic New Year and offers Muslims an opportunity for reflection, gratitude, and spiritual growth.

🌙

Sacred Month

Muharram is among the four sacred months mentioned in the Holy Quran, making it a time of peace and devotion.

🤲

Spiritual Reflection

Muslims use this month to strengthen their faith, seek forgiveness, and renew their relationship with Allah.

❤️

Charity & Kindness

Muharram encourages helping those in need through charity, compassion, and acts of kindness.

01

Muharram

Muharram is the first month of the Islamic calendar and one of the four sacred months in Islam. It marks the beginning of the Islamic New Year and encourages reflection, worship, and gratitude.

02

Safar

Safar is the second month of the Hijri calendar. Muslims continue their spiritual journey through prayer, patience, and reliance on Allah.

03

Rabi' al-Awwal

Rabi' al-Awwal is widely known as the birth month of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. Muslims remember his teachings, mercy, and exemplary character.

04

Rabi' Ul-Sani

Rabi' Ul-Sani is a month that reminds believers to continue their faith journey through worship, learning, and good deeds.

05

Jumada al-Awwal

Jumada al-Awwal is the fifth month of the Islamic calendar and encourages gratitude, perseverance, and dedication to righteous actions.

06

Jumada Ul-Sani

Jumada Ul-Sani is a time for reflection and spiritual growth, inspiring Muslims to strengthen their connection with Allah.

07

Rajab

Rajab is one of the four sacred months in Islam. It is a special period for seeking forgiveness, increasing prayers, and preparing for Ramadan.

08

Sha'ban

Sha'ban is the month before Ramadan. Muslims often increase voluntary fasting and worship to prepare themselves spiritually.

09

Ramadan

Ramadan is the holiest month in Islam. Muslims fast from dawn to sunset, recite the Quran, pray regularly, and perform acts of charity.

10

Shawwal

Shawwal begins with the celebration of Eid al-Fitr. It is a month of gratitude, joy, and continuing the good habits developed during Ramadan.

11

Dhul-Qadah

Dhul-Qadah is one of the sacred months and traditionally a time of peace. It serves as preparation for the blessed month of Hajj.

12

Dhul-Hijjah

Dhul-Hijjah is the final month of the Islamic calendar. It includes Hajj and Eid al-Adha, commemorating the devotion and sacrifice of Prophet Ibrahim (AS).

New Year Dua

Arabic Dua

اللَّهُمَّ اجْعَلْ هَذَا الْعَامَ عَامَ خَيْرٍ وَبَرَكَةٍ وَسَعَادَةٍ وَأَمْنٍ وَإِيمَانٍ

English Translation

O Allah, make this year a year of goodness, blessings, happiness, peace, and strong faith.
